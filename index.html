Cutthroat Dice Help

Cutthroat [adjective]

-'used to describe a situation in which people compete with
each other in an unpleasant and often cruel and unfair way'.
Cutthroat business practices are generally intended to drive
a competitor out of business.

Cutthroat Dice

Cutthroat Dice ... a dice game where two or more
players use the same score card, and where each player
must fend for themselves by stealing score-boxes from other
players (when allowed). Example: If player 'A' scores
three-fives, and player 'B' then rolls four-fives, player 'B'
may steal the 'fives-score' from player 'A', thus eliminating
player 'A's prior score.
The objective of Cutthroat is to generate the highest
total score by any means.

HOW TO PLAY

1. Click the "Roll Dice" button to roll the dice.
2. Lock dice that you want to keep by clicking on them.
3. Click the Roll buttom again (3 rolls total).
4. Register your score by clicking on a scoring-box.
* The game can be played by up to four players.
* Players take turns rolling five dice.
* After each roll, the player chooses which dice to keep,
  and which to re-roll.
  A player may re-roll some or all of the dice, up to two times
  on a turn (3 rolls total).
* The player must then place a score (or zero) into any
  available score-box during each turn.
* A score is available to the player if it is displaying a
  red number in a gray box.
* The game ends when all score boxes are used.
* The player with the highest score wins the game.

Scoring

Left-Section:
   Ones: The sum of all dice showing the number 1.
   Twos: The sum of all dice showing the number 2.
   Threes: The sum of all dice showing the number 3.
   Fours: The sum of all dice showing the number 4.
   Fives: The sum of all dice showing the number 5.
   Sixes: The sum of all dice showing the number 6.
   Left-Bonus: If the total score from above is greater
     than 62, the player with the most points in this section
     will be rewarded an extra 35 bonus points.

Right-Section:
 Three of a Kind: At least three dice with the same number of dots.
    Scores the sum of all dice.
 Four of a Kind: At least four dice with the same number of dots.
    Scores the sum of all dice.
 Small Straight: 1-2-3-4-?, 2-3-4-5-? or 3-4-5-6-?.
    Scores 30.
 Large Straight: 2-3-4-5-6 or 1-2-3-4-5. Scores 40.
 Full House: One pair and three of a kind,
               e.g. 3-3-3-4-4. Scores 25.
    Note: Five-of-a-kind is not accepted as a full house
     (except when a Five-of-a-kind-Bonus is used, see below).
 Chance: Any combination of dice. The score is simply the total
        of all the dice. e.g. 1-5-4-3-3 scores 16.
 Five-of-a-kind: All dice of the same value,
   e.g. 1-1-1-1-1 or 3-3-3-3-3. Will always give you 50 points.
   Five-of-a-kind-Bonus: If you get more than one Five-of-a-kind,
   you get 100 extra bonus points for each. If you have not
   scored in the Yatzy category (Yatzy = 0), you will not receive
   a bonus. You may take a bonus by clicking any unused score
   element. The value of the dice will be used to calculate the
   actual score for the element selected + the 100 bonus.
